How Junk Food Harms the Body

Excessive junk food consumption introduces high levels of sodium, trans fats, and added sugars into a developing body. This leads to several chronic conditions traditionally only seen in adults.

Fast Fact: Children who consume fast food regularly ingest approximately 187 more calories per day than those who don't, leading to significant weight gain over time.

Key Health Risks:

  • Obesity: Excessive caloric intake without nutritional value.
  • Type 2 Diabetes: Insulin resistance caused by frequent "sugar spikes."
  • Hypertension: High sodium in processed snacks leads to early heart strain.
