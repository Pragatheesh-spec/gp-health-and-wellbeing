The Double Burden

Developing nations face a cruel paradox — traditional undernutrition persists in rural areas, while urban populations adopt processed-food diets, driving obesity and diabetes simultaneously in the same country.

🏪

The Price Trap

In low-income regions, ultra-processed foods often cost a fraction of fresh produce. A bag of chips can cost less than a single apple. Economic pressure, not just preference, drives families toward junk food.

🌐

Global Food Chains

Multinational fast-food and snack brands have expanded aggressively into Southeast Asia, Latin America, and Sub-Saharan Africa — markets where nutritional regulation is still catching up to the supply.

📈

Rising Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes — once an adult lifestyle disease — is now diagnosed in children across every continent. Frequent sugar spikes from processed foods are driving insulin resistance in younger and younger populations.
