Beyond Borders

The rise of lifestyle diseases is no longer restricted to wealthy nations. As global food chains expand, developing countries are seeing a "double burden" of malnutrition and obesity.

World Health Organization (WHO): Globally, the number of overweight or obese children under the age of 5 was estimated to be over 39 million in recent years.

In developing nations, processed foods are often cheaper than fresh produce, leading to a rapid rise in diabetes in regions that previously struggled primarily with undernutrition.
